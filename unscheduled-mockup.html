Unscheduled Issues — Approach Comparison

Three UI patterns for surfacing issues that have no start/end dates in the Gantt view. Evaluate each for discoverability, visual clarity, and workflow fit.

Option A Unscheduled Tray — sticky bottom section

Pro: Unscheduled issues are always visible and clearly separated; drag-to-schedule interaction is natural.
Con: Takes up vertical space even when there are many scheduled rows; the tray area can feel disconnected from the main timeline.

Option B Ghost Bars — pinned to Today's position

Pro: Keeps all issues in context on the same timeline; visual contrast between ghost and solid bars is immediately apparent.
Con: Multiple ghost bars stacking at Today can get cluttered; users might confuse dashed bars for low-confidence estimates rather than "no date."

Option C Collapsible Sidebar Panel — grouped under Views

Pro: Keeps the timeline area clean; suits users who prefer to triage unscheduled work separately before placing it on the chart.
Con: Less discoverable — users must know to look in the sidebar; no spatial relationship between unscheduled items and the timeline.
